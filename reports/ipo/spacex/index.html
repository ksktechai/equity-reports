01

The one-paragraph version

SpaceX designs reusable rockets, runs the Starlink satellite-internet network, and — since the February 2026 xAI merger — operates an AI division (Grok, X, data-centre compute). Starlink is the profit engine; xAI is the cash drain; the valuation assumes both win.

Why investors are excited

The bull pull

Starlink scaled past 10M subscribers and threw off $4.4B operating profit in 2025. Reusable launch is a near-monopoly. Add Starship optionality and an AI-infrastructure story, and you get the most hyped listing in market history.

Why investors are worried

The bear weight

~95× sales on $18.7B revenue, a $4.9B GAAP net loss driven by xAI, ~85% Musk voting control, related-party deals with Tesla, and a thin 4.3% float with a December lock-up cliff ahead.

Is ~95× sales sane?

SpaceX has no true peer, so every comparison is imperfect. On every satcom and launch metric it trades at an enormous premium; even against high-multiple AI names it's stretched. Peer multiples below are approximate reference points, not precise live figures.

Morningstar's independent fair value sits at $63/share (~$780B) — roughly 2.5× below the ~$161 close. Two rigorous views, one paying ~$2.1T, one saying ~$780B. That gap is the investment debate.

What must be true for ~$2.1T? Starlink compounding ~40%+ for years, xAI flipping from a ~$6B loss to a profit engine, and Starship unlocking new markets — near-flawless execution across all three at once.

Governance: the weak link

A profitable core can't offset a control structure that leaves public shareholders with essentially no say. This is among the weakest governance profiles of any mega-cap.

One of the best businesses on the planet (Starlink) inside one of the most expensive and worst-governed equity structures on the planet. At ~95× sales and ~2.5× an independent fair-value mark, the price already embeds near-perfect execution across launch, telecom and AI — while the consolidated entity loses money on GAAP because of xAI. The quality is real; the entry point is the problem.

Biggest reason bulls may be right

Starlink generated $4.4B of operating profit and is the core profit centre even as the group reports a loss — a scaling, high-margin engine that could compound into the valuation over time.

Biggest reason bears may be right

Morningstar's independent fair value is $63/share versus a ~$161 close — implying the market is paying roughly 2.5× what at least one rigorous model considers fair.
