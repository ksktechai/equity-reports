01

The one-paragraph version

Salesforce sells cloud software that companies use to run sales, customer service, marketing, commerce, analytics and internal collaboration — Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, Marketing Cloud, Slack, Tableau, MuleSoft, Data Cloud and, increasingly, its Agentforce AI agents. In Q1 FY2027 (quarter ended 30 Apr 2026) revenue rose 13% to $11.13B with record operating margins, but the shares trade near a 52-week low because organic growth has slowed to ~9–10%, the market fears AI agents could erode seat-based subscriptions, and management funded a $25B accelerated buyback with new debt — converting a former net-cash balance sheet into a net-debt one and halving the free-cash-flow growth guide.

Bull thesis · one line

The bull pull

A category-defining enterprise franchise at ~11× non-GAAP earnings and an ~11% free-cash-flow yield, with Agentforce AI revenue up 205% — a cheap re-rating candidate if AI turns out to expand Salesforce's wallet rather than shrink it. Confidence: Med

Bear thesis · one line

The bear weight

Growth has decelerated to single digits and may be propped up by M&A (Informatica), while agentic AI threatens the per-seat model — and a freshly added $25B of debt removes the balance-sheet cushion just as the thesis gets riskiest. Confidence: Med

WHAT CHANGED MOST RECENTLY

Q1 FY27 beat on revenue ($11.13B, +13%) and EPS (non-GAAP $3.88, +50%; GAAP $2.42, +52%), with non-GAAP operating margin at a record 34.8% and Agentforce reaching ~$1.2B annualized revenue. DO ECONOMICS IMPROVE WITH SCALE?

Yes on cost — gross margin has climbed from ~73% (FY22) to ~78% and non-GAAP operating margin hit a record 34.8% last quarter as the post-2023 efficiency drive stuck. The unresolved question is on the top line: the historic engine was selling more seats at higher prices, and Agentforce's consumption (per-conversation) pricing is a deliberate hedge against AI reducing the number of human seats customers need. Net revenue retention, once well above 120%, has drifted toward ~%-low-teens growth. Confidence: Med

Governance & management

Founder-led with a single class of stock — public holders' votes count fully — but with a long, mixed record on big acquisitions and a recent history of activist intervention that reshaped how the company is run.

Strengths

What's good

No dual-class shares — one share, one vote. Marc Benioff (co-founder, Chair & CEO since 1999) is a genuine product visionary who built the category. After activists (Elliott, Starboard and others) pushed in 2023, margins roughly doubled and the company embraced buybacks and a dividend — a real, value-creating course-correction.

Watch items

What to watch

Combined Chair/CEO concentrates power, and insider ownership is modest (~3%). The M&A record is mixed: Slack ($27.7B) and Tableau ($15.7B) drew criticism on price, and the company keeps acquiring (Informatica). Co-CEO and senior-leadership turnover has been notable. The debt-funded buyback is itself a capital-allocation call to judge. Governance Score: 7 / 10.

A contrarian, valuation-driven call — not a quality call. The overall score is middling because growth has genuinely slowed and the balance sheet just took on leverage. But at ~11× non-GAAP earnings, an ~11% FCF yield, 78% gross margins and a 52-week low, the asymmetry favors patient buyers if agentic AI proves additive rather than cannibalistic — which is the single swing factor for the whole thesis. This is explicitly not personalised advice and we are not licensed advisors; a reader who believes AI will erode the seat model should treat this as "Neutral."

Biggest reason bulls may be right

You're buying the system of record for enterprise customer data — the layer AI agents need to act on — at half its historical multiple and an ~11% FCF yield. If Agentforce turns AI into a wallet-share expander, both earnings and the multiple can re-rate from a very low base.

Biggest reason bears may be right

The entire model is priced per human seat, and the clearest use of agentic AI is to need fewer of them. If that plays out, revenue decays structurally while ~$25B of new debt sits on the books — a cheap multiple on a shrinking base is a value trap, not a bargain.
