01

The one-paragraph version

Nebius is a vertically-integrated AI cloud: it builds and operates data centres, fills them with Nvidia GPUs, and rents that compute to AI labs and enterprises — the "neocloud" model, alongside CoreWeave. It is the surviving international rump of Yandex N.V., which sold its Russian businesses in July 2024 and relisted on Nasdaq as Nebius on 21 Oct 2024. Revenue is inflecting violently: FY2024 ~$117.5M → FY2025 $529.8M (+351%) → Q1 2026 $399M (+684% YoY), with core AI-cloud annualised run-rate (ARR) hitting ~$1.92B at quarter-end. Management guides 2026 group revenue of $3.0–3.4B and exit ARR of $7–9B, ~40% adjusted-EBITDA margin — and has raised 2026 capex to a staggering $20–25B. The stock has ~6×'d in a year to a ~$72B market cap.

WHAT CHANGED MOST RECENTLY

A cluster of events re-rated the stock in 2026: Nvidia disclosed a $2B equity investment at $94.94/share (~8.3% stake) in March; Nebius then signed a Microsoft GPU-capacity contract valued at ~$17.4–19.4B (anchored by a new Vineland, NJ site) and a ~$3B Meta deal; Q1 2026 results (reported 13 May) showed revenue +684% and the first positive adjusted EBITDA; and the company secured over 3.5 GW of power, including a new 1.2 GW owned site in Pennsylvania. It also closed the Eigen AI acquisition on 10 Jun 2026. Shares trade near all-time highs.

DO ECONOMICS IMPROVE WITH SCALE?

Yes, in the near term — and that is the difference from a year ago. Adjusted EBITDA swung to +$129.5M in Q1 2026 from a ~$53.7M loss a year earlier, and management guides ~40% group adjusted-EBITDA margin for 2026. THE NUMBER THAT MATTERS MOST — CAPEX vs REVENUE

Nebius lifted its 2026 capex guidance to $20–25B against guided 2026 revenue of just $3.0–3.4B — i.e. it plans to spend roughly 6–8× its annual revenue building capacity this year alone. Even with ~$9.3B of cash and ~$6B of monetisable ClickHouse stake, that gap must be bridged with substantial new debt, equity, or customer prepayments. The bull reads this as demand-driven scaling; the bear reads it as a balance sheet betting the company on the AI-capex cycle not turning.

Governance & management

A proven builder-CEO with deep skin in the game, wrapped in a Dutch holding-company structure, a dual-class vote, and a complicated post-Yandex history that demands more diligence than a typical US-listed name.

Strengths

What's good

Founder-CEO Arkady Volozh built Yandex into one of the world's few non-US/China search-and-tech champions, and personally owns ~11.53% of Nebius (~$6.25B) — alignment is very high. The team is ~1,000+ ex-Yandex engineers with real infrastructure pedigree, and Nvidia's $2B investment is a strategic endorsement few neoclouds can claim. Management has been disciplined about "crystallising" stake value to self-fund rather than purely diluting.

Watch items

What to watch

Dual-class Class A/B structure concentrates voting power with insiders; the Dutch (N.V.) domicile and holding-company web (Avride, TripleTen, ClickHouse, Toloka) reduce transparency vs a clean pure-play. The Yandex legacy — sanctions-era trading suspension (2022), the Russian-asset divestiture (2024) and Volozh's own prior EU-sanctions history (since delisted) — is a reputational and headline risk even though the Russian operations are gone. Governance Score: 4 / 10. Confidence: Med

A "great business, demanding price, brutal capital math" call. Nebius is arguably the best-positioned independent neocloud — Nvidia-backed, hyper-growing, EBITDA-positive, with marquee Microsoft and Meta contracts and a stash of monetisable stakes. Those are the reasons the score isn't lower. But it trades at ~75–80× trailing sales above analyst consensus, must finance a $20–25B build that dwarfs its revenue, and its reported profit leans on fair-value gains rather than cloud free cash flow. The whole thesis hinges on the AI-capex cycle staying hot while it converts contracted ARR into durable cash. This is explicitly not personalised advice and we are not licensed advisors; a believer in the ramp and Nvidia alignment could rate this "Buy," while a skeptic on capex, dilution and the cycle could rate it "Sell."

Biggest reason bulls may be right

If AI compute demand keeps outstripping supply, Nebius becomes the scarce, investable independent neocloud — Nvidia-blessed, contracted to Microsoft and Meta, and sitting on billions in sellable stakes — and a $1.9B ARR base compounds toward $7–9B and beyond, making today's multiple look cheap in hindsight.

Biggest reason bears may be right

The market is paying ~75–80× sales for a capital-devouring landlord of depreciating rented GPUs that must raise tens of billions to grow into its valuation. If the AI-capex cycle cools or financing tightens, EBITDA optics won't save free cash flow, and a stock trading above consensus has a long way to fall.
