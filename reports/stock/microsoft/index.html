01

The one-paragraph version

Microsoft sells software, cloud infrastructure, and productivity tools to nearly every large enterprise on the planet — Azure, Microsoft 365, Windows, LinkedIn, Dynamics, GitHub and Xbox. In its most recent quarter (FQ3 FY2026, ended 31 Mar 2026) revenue rose 18% to $82.9B and Azure grew ~40%, yet the shares are down sharply over the past year because management committed to ~$190B of FY2026 capital spending to build AI capacity, pressuring free cash flow and raising the question of when — and whether — that spend earns its cost of capital.

Bull thesis · one line

The bull pull

A fortress franchise with ~70% gross margins and $70B+ of free cash flow is monetising AI across Azure and Copilot while the multiple has compressed to ~23× — below its own five-year norm and a discount to mega-cap peers. Confidence: Med

Bear thesis · one line

The bear weight

A ~$190B annual capex ramp converts a famously cash-generative business into a capital-intensive one, with rising depreciation, uncertain AI ROI, and a still-rich absolute valuation for a company that can't compound at 30%+ forever. Confidence: Med

WHAT CHANGED MOST RECENTLY

FQ3 beat on revenue ($82.9B vs ~$81.5B expected) and EPS ($4.27, +23%), and Microsoft Cloud crossed $50B in a quarter for the first time ($54.5B, +29%). But the FY capex guide of ~$190B — driven partly by soaring memory costs — overshadowed the beat and weighed on the stock.

06

Governance & management

Among the cleaner governance profiles in mega-cap tech: a single class of stock (one share, one vote), an independent board, and a CEO with an exceptional capital-allocation record — offset by the concentration of Chair and CEO in one person.

Strengths

What's good

No dual-class shares — public holders' votes count fully. Satya Nadella (CEO since 2014) engineered the cloud pivot and the early OpenAI partnership; capital allocation (Azure, GitHub, LinkedIn, the $69B Activision deal) has largely created value. Mostly independent board.

Watch items

What to watch

Nadella holds both Chair and CEO roles, concentrating power. Insider ownership is minimal (typical for a professionally-managed mega-cap). The single biggest capital-allocation test of the era — the ~$190B AI capex bet — is still unproven. Governance Score: 8 / 10.
